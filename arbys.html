• Roast Beef

    There's nothing quite as satisfying as the classics.

    You should always remember your roots, and for that there's the Roast Beef. Humble in appearance, this pile of meat will fill you up yet strangely leave you wanting more. This is how it all begins, and if you've never experienced Arby's, start here.

  • Beef and Cheddar

    Perhaps the greatest invention since the Roast Beef was the addition of cheese and ranch dressing.

    The Beef and Cheddar is saucy and zippy. Mix in some Horsey Sauce for some unexpected zip, or just dip it in your Arby's Sauce for some tang. It's okay, your curly fries won't mind.

  • Bacon Beef and Cheddar

    This sandwich makes grown men cry.

    Pepper bacon takes already perhaps the greatest thing you can hold in your hand and takes it up a notch. It's bacon, it's beef, it's cheddar, and eating it is like kissing a newborn baby. The happiness that fills you will last for weeks.

  • Classic Melt

    Forget the regular "melt", beef makes a delicious melt that will have tuna on the sidelines crying for his mommy.

    It's not a beef and cheddar, as it lacks the ranch dressing, but it's exactly what you want in your sandwich - meat and zesty cheddar sauce.

  • French Dip, Melted Swiss

    You're a rebel and we love you for it.

    The french dip gets a touch of "melt" with a generous portion of swiss cheese to join you on your decent into Au Jus delight. When you're done, drink the juice! In some states, we believe it should be a crime to leave any of the French Dip behind.

  • Reuben

    Every class of sandwich deserves a title and a crown.

    The epic reuben is the only sandwich known to mankind to deserve the word epic (typically reserved for things like galaxies and oceans). One bite of this delicious corned beef and you'll lose yourself in flavor nirvana. It doesn't smell like teen spirit, it smells like Arby's!

  • Roast Turkey Ranch and Bacon Wrap

    Market Fresh doesn't mean you have to give up flavor.

    The Roast Turkey is piled high, and you know it's healthy because it's turkey. Ripe tomatoes and whole grain bread make this the go-to item if you feel you can't handle the more awesome beef based sandwiches. We promise not to laugh. Much.

  • Roast Turkey and Swiss

    Even lighter than the Roast Turkey Ranch is a turkey and swiss.

    Arby's does it right, not skimping on the sandwich but still delivering flavor in every bite. Like all Market Fresh items, this too has 100% whole grain. Like all Market Fresh items, we'll give you a hard time for ordering it.

  • Farmhouse Salad

    You're really desperate to get out of here on the healthy side!

    The Farmhouse Salad is exactly what it's name says- a salad. Crispy chicken, thick ranch dressing, and a pile of produce will have you soon realizing that while this says "salad" in its name, it's actually a pile of sandwich goodies tossed in a bowl.

Steeped in Tradition

Your first lunch at a company should be memorable. It's not good enough to visit the local eatery- for many years, LinkedIn would take new hires to Arby's, home of the Roast Beef Sandwich. Just one look at the dizzying array of sandwiches above should tell you why this is a favorite of Web Development shops who's name end in "kedIn". Like Web Development here at LinkedIn, Arby's too is full of tradition

It started in 1965 in Arkon, Ohio with a logo that resembled a 10-gallon hat. By 1975 the hat we known and love was the logo of choice; by 1980 over 1,000 restaurants existed around the country. The silicon valley was blessed with not one but two Arby's franchises in the first 15 years. The 256th Arby's (that's 2^8 for all you who believe this was meant to be) was opened on South Bernardo in Sunnyvale, CA. The rest, as they say, is history.

Our Arby's

Arby's Franchise 256 is located at 601 S Bernardo Ave in Sunnyvale, CA. When you're in a rush from LinkedIn, the fastest way there is highway 85 South, exiting at El Camino Real (highway 82) South. Though it might seem like a long drive on El Camino Real, hang in there and follow your nose. The Arby's is on the right hand side, at the corner of El Camino Real and S Bernardo Ave.

Returning from Arby's is difficult; not because of the directions, but because of how full of happiness and joy you will be. Either make a U-turn on El Camino Real or a left turn from S Bernardo Ave to begin traveling North on El Camino Real. Take the 85 North onramp on the righthand side, and exit Shoreline. From here, returning to work should be easy.

Warning: Beyond the Arby's, there's this other restaurant called Adamsons. While they have excellent French Dip sandwiches, don't be fooled! It smells like Arby's, it might even look a bit like Arby's, but it most assuredly is not. When you want Arby's, be sure to stop at the Arby's and don't go further.
